# Testimony of Archivist Rowan

Recorded 11 days after the disappearance.

For the record:

Elias Voss was a good librarian.

He was also a terrible liar.

---

Everyone keeps asking about the eastern wing.

Everyone keeps asking about the book.

Nobody asks the obvious question.

Why was Elias so afraid?

---

I worked beside him for six years.

I watched him memorize entire shelves.

I watched him restore books that were older than kingdoms.

I watched him speak to visitors as though every question mattered.

Then I watched him change.

---

The first change happened after the excavation.

Nobody talks about that anymore.

Most records were destroyed.

Some vanished entirely.

But I remember.

The workers discovered a stone chamber beneath the library.

The chamber contained only three things:

* a locked door
* a wooden chair
* a book resting on the floor

The book should not have survived.

Everything else was dust.

---

People think Elias found the book.

That is not true.

The book found Elias.

---

Three nights after he brought it upstairs, the library began changing.

Books moved.

Rooms shifted.

Visitors reported getting lost in familiar hallways.

Some claimed they entered sections of the library that did not exist the day before.

---

One visitor disappeared.

The records say otherwise.

The records are wrong.

I remember her.

Nobody else does.

Not anymore.

---

Elias believed the book was consuming knowledge.

I believed something worse.

I believed it was consuming memory.

---

On the final night I saw him.

He stood outside the lower door.

The book was open.

The library was silent.

Not normal silence.

The kind of silence that happens when something is listening.

---

He looked at me and said:

"Rowan, if I fail, remember me."

Then he closed the door.

I never saw him again.

---
