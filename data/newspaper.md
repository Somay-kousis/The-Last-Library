# The Chronicle

## Local Library Closed Following Structural Concerns

**Published: October 17**

The City Archives Committee has announced the indefinite closure of the Old Meridian Library following concerns regarding structural instability in the eastern section of the building.

Officials cited deteriorating foundations, unsafe corridors, and the discovery of previously undocumented underground chambers as reasons for the closure.

Head Librarian **Elias Voss** declined to comment on the findings.

Visitors have reported seeing maintenance crews entering and exiting the property throughout the week, though city representatives insist no unusual activity has occurred.

Several rumors have circulated online regarding:

* missing records
* restricted access areas
* unexplained sounds after closing hours
* the disappearance of a visitor

Authorities have dismissed these claims as misinformation.

"There is no evidence that any visitor has gone missing," stated committee spokesperson Mara Quinn.

The library's collection is expected to be transferred to temporary storage while inspectors complete their investigation.

When asked about reports concerning a sealed staircase discovered beneath the eastern wing, officials declined further comment.

The building will remain closed until further notice.

---

### Public Notice

Citizens are reminded that entering the library grounds without authorization is prohibited.

Any documents, journals, letters, or artifacts recovered from the property should be reported to city authorities immediately.
