# Journal of Elias Voss

## Head Librarian

Entry 218

There is a comfort in routine.

The morning catalog.
The afternoon repairs.
The evening silence.

The library has survived for centuries because every librarian before me understood one thing:

Knowledge should be protected.

Not all knowledge should be found.

---

Entry 224

The sounds beneath the eastern wing have returned.

At first I believed them to be settling stone.

Now I am less certain.

The sounds have rhythm.

Almost like breathing.

---

Entry 227

Today a book appeared on my desk.

No catalog number.
No author.
No title.

It should not exist.

I locked it beneath the library.

---

Entry 229

The book has begun moving.

Not physically.

Within itself.

Pages contain different text each morning.

Names appear and disappear.

Entire chapters rewrite themselves.

Yesterday I found my own name inside.

Today I found the names of people who have not yet entered the library.

I have stopped sleeping.

---

Entry 231

Archivist Rowan asked what was hidden beneath the eastern wing.

I lied.

May the library forgive me.

---

Entry 234

I finally opened the book.

I wish I had not.

Everything happening now began after that moment.

The whispers.
The missing records.
The changes.

The book does not contain knowledge.

It consumes it.

---

Final Entry

If you are reading this, then I have failed.

Do not search for the book.

Do not open the lower door.

And if someone tells you the library vanished...

Do not believe them.

The library is still here.

Something else is missing.
