# Report on the Closure of the Eastern Wing

Archive Reference: EW-17

Date: Three Days Before the Disappearance

By order of the Head Librarian, public access to the Eastern Wing has been suspended indefinitely.

No explanation has been provided to visitors.

Several staff members have expressed concern regarding unusual sounds reported during the night shift.

Descriptions include:

* pages turning in empty rooms
* distant footsteps in sealed corridors
* books appearing in locations where they were not previously catalogued
* whispers heard between shelves after closing hours

An inspection team was dispatched on the second evening.

Their official report contains only one sentence:

"We recommend the immediate closure of the wing."

No further details were attached.

One item recovered from the inspection was a torn note found beneath Shelf E-42.

The note contained the following:

"The door below is no longer staying closed."

The author remains unidentified.

---

Witness Statement

A junior archivist claimed to have seen the Head Librarian carrying a lantern toward the restricted staircase shortly before midnight.

The staircase leads beneath the library.

Access records for that area have since disappeared.
