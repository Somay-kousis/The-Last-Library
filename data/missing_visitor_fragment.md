My name is Elara Finch.

If this document still exists, then the book has not finished its work.

I am writing this because nobody remembers me anymore.

Not Rowan.

Not Elias.

Not even myself, some days.

---

I was never a visitor.

That was the first lie.

I worked in the library.

I lived in the library.

I knew every corridor, every hidden staircase, every forgotten room.

I even knew what waited beneath the eastern wing.

---

The book was already awake when they found it.

---

No.

That is not true.

The book was never asleep.

---

The first thing it took was a memory.

A small one.

A child's name from a visitor log.

Nobody noticed.

Then it took another.

And another.

The losses were tiny.

Almost impossible to detect.

---

The danger was not that it erased knowledge.

The danger was that it rewrote absence.

People did not remember forgetting.

They remembered a world where the forgotten thing had never existed.

---

I was the first person to understand this.

That is why the book chose me.

---

I should not have opened it.

I should not have read the final page.

I should not have learned what lives inside.

---

The final page contains a list.

The list changes.

Every morning it contains different names.

Every evening the list grows.

---

At first the names belonged to strangers.

Then librarians.

Then visitors.

Then friends.

---

Then mine.

---

I told Elias.

He promised he would destroy the book.

He lied.

---

I told Rowan.

He promised he would remember me.

He failed.

---

I am writing this because someone will eventually ask the correct question.

When they do, this fragment will be found.

If that person is you, then listen carefully.

The library did not vanish.

Elias did not vanish.

The missing people did not vanish.

---

They were moved.
